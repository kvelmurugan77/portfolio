Operational EYA Project Setup

Configure the wind farm Operational Energy Yield Assessment (OEYA) context. This tool replicates the methodology of a lender/owner's-engineer Operational Energy Production Report (OEPR) — availability per IEC 61400-26, plant power curve & MCP against long-term reference wind speed, LT gross & net energy, RSS uncertainty combination, and probability-of-exceedance (P50/P75/P90/P95/P99) per DNV-RP-0126 / ISO GUM. All data stays in your browser — nothing is uploaded to a server. Outputs are saved as local files (CSV, PNG, JSON, HTML report).

Methodology — Operational EYA Workflow
Stage A — Data QA

1. SCADA ingest with timestamp auto-detection (dayfirst vs monthfirst) — prevents the silent scrambling bug that broke earlier SCADA pipelines.

2. Per-turbine monthly data-completeness check (IEC 61400-26 data coverage requirement).

3. Status code → IEC category mapping (full / partial / unavailable / unknown).

Stage B — Plant OEYA Core

4. Per-turbine auto power curve (clean-status binning, monotonic smoothing).

5. IEC 61400-26 energy-based availability: Avail = 1 − Unavail_E / (Avail_E + Unavail_E).

6. Plant power curve + MCP regression against LT reference wind speed.

7. LT gross AEP, normalized to 30-day months, then back to calendar months.

Stage C — Uncertainty & POE

8. RSS uncertainty combination across 9 standard sources (power measurement, air density, historical representativeness, future wind, wind freq distribution, future availability, curtailment, electrical, blade degradation).

9. P50 / P75 / P90 / P95 / P99 via normal distribution z-scores (ISO GUM).

10. Gross-to-Net waterfall + optional EYA-vs-OA reconciliation.

11. Yaw misalignment & wind-speed sensor cross-check diagnostics.

Status Code → IEC 61400-26 Category Mapping

IEC 61400-26 classifies each SCADA record into four energy-based categories: FULL (unconstrained normal operation), PARTIAL (derated/curtailed but producing), UNAVAIL (forced or scheduled outage), UNKNOWN (cannot classify). The OEYA energy-based availability uses Possible Power from auto-curve (or OEM signal) for UNAVAIL periods and Actual Power for AVAIL periods.

IEC 61400-26 Energy-Based Availability

For each turbine, an empirical "auto power curve" is built from FULL-status records (clean, unconstrained operation) by binning power output vs wind speed. For every SCADA record, the theoretical ("potential") power is then computed from the auto-curve at the observed wind speed. Availability = 1 − Unavailable Energy / (Available Energy + Unavailable Energy), where Available Energy is the actual energy in available periods and Unavailable Energy is the theoretical energy lost during unavailable periods. Unknown periods are excluded entirely.

Plant & Per-Turbine Power Curves

Plant power curve aggregates all FULL-status records across the fleet, binned at 0.5 m/s resolution. Bins with fewer than 6 samples are dropped and the curve is enforced monotonically non-decreasing via cumulative-max smoothing (replicates oepr_availability.build_auto_curve). The plant curve feeds the MCP regression in the next step.

Long-Term Wind Reference & MCP Regression

Measure-Correlate-Predict (MCP) regressions the plant's monthly gross energy (normalised to 30-day months, Eq. 2-3 of the OEPR methodology) against a long-term reference wind speed series over the operational period, then applies the regression to the full LT reference record to predict long-term gross AEP. The standard OEPR convention uses Eq. 5: Prod_norm = a × WS_ref + b. Air density adjustment is applied first (Eq. 4: Vn = Vobs × (ρ/ρ₀)^(1/3)) if a density series is supplied.

Long-Term Gross AEP Climatology
Gross → Net Energy Reconciliation & Losses

Long-term Gross AEP is bridged to P50 Net AEP via the standard OEPR loss stack: turbine availability, BoP availability, curtailment, electrical, and blade degradation. Each loss is applied multiplicatively in the standard DNV/UL convention. Optionally, a pre-construction EYA-vs-OA reconciliation waterfall can be built with named components; any gap is auto-assigned to an "Unexplained" bucket per DNV practice.

Uncertainty Combination (RSS) & Probability of Exceedance

Following ISO GUM / DNV-RP-0126, individual uncertainty sources are combined in root-sum-square (RSS): U_combined = √(Σ U_i²). Two periods are reported — Eval Period (operational POR uncertainty) and Annual Period (long-term, with elevated future wind-resource uncertainty). Probability-of-exceedance (POE) values are computed assuming a normal distribution: P_x = P50 × (1 + z_x × U/100) where z_x = −Φ⁻¹(x/100).

Yaw Misalignment Screening

Two complementary checks: (1) Static offset — circular mean of (nacelle_direction − wind_direction) during power-producing operation; a well-aligned turbine should average ~0°. (2) Power-vs-offset peak — bin power by the offset and fit a quadratic to find the peak; if the peak is shifted away from zero, the turbine is consistently misaligned with the wind. The static check drives the automated flag (|mean| > 5°); the peak is provided for engineer review.

Wind Speed Sensor Cross-Check

Each turbine's anemometer reading is compared against the concurrent plant-consensus median (all OTHER turbines at the same timestamp). A turbine with |bias| > 0.5 m/s may be miscalibrated or drifting; high scatter (>1.5 m/s) with near-zero bias suggests intermittent noise or icing.
