Technical Due Diligence Checklist

Systematically evaluate the WTG type against industry standards, certification requirements, and project-specific criteria. Each item should be assessed as Pass, Fail, Conditional, or N/A. Findings from failed or conditional items should be documented in Step 3.

Findings & Recommendations

Document all findings from the Technical Due Diligence evaluation. Categorise each finding by severity, link to the relevant checklist item, and provide clear recommendations for resolution. Critical and Major findings require mandatory resolution before procurement close-out.

Employer's Technical Requirements (ETR)

Develop the Employer's Technical Requirements document tailored to the specific project. The ETR defines the minimum technical requirements that WTG suppliers must comply with, and serves as the baseline for evaluating supplier deviations during procurement. Each section can be customised to suit project-specific conditions.

WTG Supplier Deviation Management

During the project procurement phase, manage the close-out of any WTG supplier deviations to the Employer's Technical Requirements. Ensure the relevant technical expertise is consulted on each point of deviation to reach closure. Any associated residual risks must be implemented in the project risk register (Step 6).
